**The ranking has a real but modest edge over the full cycle.** Win% (63.8→70.5%) and
Med% (+12.8→+20.1%) rise monotonically as the slice tightens, and Mean% improves
(+26.7→+31.3%). So the score ordering carries genuine information — it is not noise.

**But the magnitude is far smaller than 2021–2026 implied.** Sortino climbs only
0.831 → 0.947 across the full cut (vs ~2.3 in the 2024–2026 test window). Tail risk is
**not** reduced — CVaR stays ≈ −62% at every slice. The dramatic recent outperformance
was substantially regime-specific.

**The edge cannot be had at the ~3/month target.** Meaningful improvement only appears
at top-33%/25%, which run at **0.7–0.9 trades/month** — roughly one trade every 5–6
weeks. At ~3/mo (≈ top-75% / all) the ranking adds essentially nothing.

**Yearly consistency does not improve** with tightening (Yrs+ ≈ 83–88% throughout), so
the score concentrates returns into fewer trades rather than removing bad years.

### Bottom line
Use the freshness score as a **soft quality tilt** (prefer higher-scoring breakouts when
sizing or when more candidates fire than you can take), not as a hard filter. As a hard
filter it forces a trade-off: keep ~3/mo and get no edge, or chase the edge and drop to
<1/mo. The 2024–2026 strength should be treated as regime-aided, not a stable alpha.
